What is BMI?

Body Mass Index estimates body fat based on height and weight. It's a quick screening tool, not a diagnosis — muscle mass, bone density and body composition all affect the true picture.

  • Underweight — below 18.5
  • Normal — 18.5 to 24.9
  • Overweight — 25 to 29.9
  • Obese — 30 and above

Mifflin-St Jeor Formula

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, widely regarded as the most accurate BMR formula for most adults:

Men: (10 × weight) + (6.25 × height) − (5 × age) + 5
Women: (10 × weight) + (6.25 × height) − (5 × age) − 161

How This Works

Your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) is your BMR multiplied by an activity factor. To lose weight, eat below TDEE; to gain, eat above it; to maintain, match it.
